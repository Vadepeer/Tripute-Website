A. P. J Abdul Kalam 1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become one of the most respected and inspirational figures in India. Dr. Kalam is best known for his pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. His work in the development of ballistic missile technology and the launch vehicle technology earned him the nickname "Missile Man of India." He played a crucial role in the successful testing of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which significantly strengthened India's defense capabilities

Biographies

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. Studied physics and aerospace engineering.
  • Known as the "Missile Man of India" for developing key missiles like Agni and Prithvi.
  • Worked on India’s space program and helped develop satellite launch vehicles.
  • Played a major role in India’s successful nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Served as Chief Scientific Adviser and led the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • Was the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, known as the "People’s President."
  • Passionate about education and inspired many through his autobiography, "Wings of Fire."
  • Died on July 27, 2015, while giving a lecture. Remembered as a visionary and beloved leader.
